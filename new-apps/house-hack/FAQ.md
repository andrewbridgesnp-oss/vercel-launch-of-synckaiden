### What is KAIDEN HouseHack 203K?
A complete SaaS platform that guides buyers through FHA 203(k) renovation loans for 1-4 unit house-hacking properties. Think TurboTax for FHA 203(k) deals - step-by-step guidance, calculators, team collaboration, and deal management.

### Who is this for?
- **First-time home buyers** exploring house-hacking with FHA 203(k) loans
- **Real estate investors** buying 2-4 unit properties with renovation financing
- **Real estate agents** helping clients navigate 203(k) deals
- **Loan officers** managing 203(k) loan pipelines
- **203(k) consultants** coordinating rehab projects
- **Contractors** bidding on 203(k) renovation work

### What's house-hacking?
Buying a 2-4 unit property, living in one unit, and renting the others. Rental income offsets (or covers) your mortgage, reducing your housing expense. FHA 203(k) lets you buy AND renovate with one loan and only 3.5% down.

### Is this a mortgage lender?
**No.** We're an educational platform and organizational tool. We don't lend money, provide specific rate quotes, or guarantee loan approval. You'll work with your own FHA-approved lender.

## üèóÔ∏è FHA 203(K) LOAN SPECIFICS

### What's the difference between Limited and Standard 203(k)?
**Limited 203(k):**
- Max $75,000 in rehab costs (as of 11/04/2024)
- Simpler projects (cosmetic, minor repairs)
- No HUD consultant required
- Faster process (9 months max)

**Standard 203(k):**
- No maximum rehab cost (subject to loan limits)
- Structural work allowed (foundation, additions)
- HUD consultant required
- Longer timeframe (12 months max)

### How much down payment do I need?
**3.5% minimum** for FHA loans (if credit score is 580+). For example:
- Purchase price: $200,000
- Rehab costs: $50,000
- Total loan: $250,000
- Down payment: $8,750 (3.5%)

### Can I do the work myself?
Limited DIY is allowed, but you can't pay yourself. You must hire licensed contractors for most work. Consult your lender and 203(k) consultant for specifics.

### What property types qualify?
- 1-4 unit residential properties
- Single-family homes, duplexes, triplexes, fourplexes
- Must be owner-occupied (you must live there)
- Condos (if FHA-approved)

**Not eligible:**
- 5+ unit properties
- Commercial buildings
- Investment properties (no owner occupancy)
- Vacation homes

### What repairs are allowed?
**Allowed:**
- Structural repairs (foundation, roof)
- Cosmetic updates (paint, flooring)
- Kitchen/bathroom remodels
- HVAC, plumbing, electrical
- Accessibility modifications
- Energy efficiency upgrades

**Not allowed:**
- Luxury items (pools, outdoor kitchens)
- "Delayed improvements" (work after 12 months)
- Commercial-use spaces
